I've read and been told that writing and blogging is a really helpful way to cement what you're working on or learning. After saying that I was going to commit a little more time to this in 2021 and missing that goal miserably, I thought we'd try again for 2022. I'm setting what feels like an achievable goal of a post per month, and not to anything in particular. It will likely have some form of software/machine learning/infrastructure/biology theme, but nothing overly strict. And I expect it to mostly be on projects rather than thought/opinion pieces; feel like there's probably enough of that available on the internet. This may change as the year goes by and I get a little more comfortable with writing things down, so we'll just see what happens. 

A little about me; I work as a software & ML engineer at the biotech start-up "Antiverse". This is the first time I've ever been called a "software engineer" having progressed through various titles (Data Analytics boy, Data Scientist, machine learning engineer etc). Whilst I do have a bunch of experience which certainly qualifies me to claim the title "software engineer", there's a whole plethora of things that I either:

A) dont know, 

B) have a good appreciation of 

C) don't do very well

and that's largely what this blog is to help solve. Or at least to improve a few of things. 

I hope it's useful or interesting to somebody else too. If you've got questions or comments, then honestly twitter is probably the best place to send them (I don't have a comments section setup here, it's not that sophisticated!). 

Thanks, Joe.
